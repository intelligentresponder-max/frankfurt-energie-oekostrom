New to Germany? Here's how the energy market works

In Germany, every household has the legal right to freely choose their electricity and gas supplier. When you move in, you are automatically assigned to the local default supplier (Grundversorger) – which is typically the most expensive option on the market.

Frequently asked questions

Is switching electricity providers complicated?

Not at all. You only need your last annual bill and your meter number. The entire process takes under 10 minutes. Your new provider handles the cancellation – your power is never cut off.

Is green electricity really cheaper than the default supplier?

Yes – in most Frankfurt postcodes our certified green tariffs are significantly cheaper than the local default supplier (Mainova). Our best current tariff: 31.90 ct/kWh vs. ~41 ct/kWh at Mainova.

What documents do I need?

Just your last electricity bill (showing your annual consumption and meter number) and your bank details for the SEPA direct debit. That's it.

What is a "Grundversorger" (default supplier)?

In Germany, every region has a legally designated default electricity supplier. If you haven't actively chosen a provider, you're automatically supplied by them – usually at the highest rates on the market. Switching is free and takes minutes.

Can I get a switching bonus?

Yes. Depending on the tariff, welcome bonuses of up to €100 are available. This increases your effective first-year saving to up to €179. We'll show you all current bonus offers.
